How it works

Adaptive
Each section has two modules. Module 1 is a mix of easy, medium, and hard questions; how you do on it decides whether Module 2 is harder (higher scoring ceiling) or easier. Time is per module — unused minutes don't carry over, and a finished module locks.
No penalty
Wrong answers cost nothing, so never leave a question blank — guess if you must.
Pretest items
4 of the 98 questions (2 per section) are unscored field-test questions. You can't tell which, so treat every question as if it counts.
Built-in tools
On-screen Desmos graphing calculator (all of Math), highlight/annotate, flag-for-review, and a math reference sheet.
Seat time
Plan ~3 hours at the center once check-in and setup are included.

Reading & Writing 64 min · 54 Q

Two 27-question modules, 32 minutes each (~71 seconds per question). One short passage (25–150 words) per question — topics span literature, history/social studies, science, and the humanities. Four domains, split fairly evenly:

Information & Ideas

Central ideas, details, inference, and command of evidence — including charts and graphs.

Craft & Structure

Vocabulary in context, a text's structure and purpose, and connecting paired passages.

Expression of Ideas

Revising for clarity and rhetorical goals, plus "rhetorical synthesis" — turning notes into the best sentence.

Standard English Conventions

Grammar, punctuation, and sentence boundaries.

Focus first: grammar/punctuation rules and vocabulary-in-context are the fastest, most reliable points. Transitions and rhetorical synthesis show up a lot and are very learnable.

Math 70 min · 44 Q

Two 22-question modules, 35 minutes each (~95 seconds per question). About three-quarters are multiple choice; the rest are "student-produced response" — you type the answer. The Desmos calculator is allowed the entire time. Four domains:

Algebra

Linear equations, inequalities, and systems — the largest share of questions.

Advanced Math

Quadratics, exponentials, and functions / nonlinear relationships.

Problem-Solving & Data Analysis

Ratios, percentages, rates, probability, statistics, and reading graphs/tables.

Geometry & Trigonometry

Angles, area and volume, circles, and right-triangle trig.

Focus first: Algebra and Advanced Math make up most of the points — master those before anything else, and get fluent with Desmos to solve and graph fast while avoiding arithmetic slips.

Scoring & targets

Total
400–1600 = Reading & Writing (200–800) + Math (200–800), with domain subscores in your report.
Results
Usually released about two weeks after test day.
Readiness
College Board's college-ready benchmarks are roughly 480 (R&W) and 530 (Math).

Approximate national score ladder (percentiles shift year to year — treat as a rough guide):

~1050around the national average
~1200roughly top third
~1350roughly top 10%
~1450roughly top 5%
1500+roughly top 1–2%
Set your real target this way: look up the middle-50% SAT range for each college on Emi's list (on the school's admissions page or Common Data Set) and aim at or above its 75th-percentile number. That's far more useful than any single national figure.

What to focus on, test day

Be clean in Module 1.

Accuracy early routes you to the higher-scoring Module 2 — don't rush to "bank time."

Pace by module, not by question.

Flag a hard one, move on, and circle back before the module locks.

Answer everything.

There's no penalty for wrong answers.

Know the tools beforehand.

Practice Desmos, highlighting, and flagging in Bluebook so test day feels familiar.

Bring a charged device + charger.

A dead battery mid-module can end the test.

Reference figures reflect the current digital SAT. Always confirm specifics on College Board's site.
